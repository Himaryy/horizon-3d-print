# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two equal audiences:

**Collectors (18-35):** Indonesian hobbyists who know articulated figures, poseable toys, and 3D printing. They land knowing what they want, scan fast, judge quality by detail and craft. They want to see the product clearly, understand variants and stock, and buy without friction.

**Gift buyers (parents, friends):** Casual visitors, no knowledge of 3D printing. They need delight and social proof fast. They buy on feeling: "this looks cool, unique, Indonesian-made, my kid will love it."

Both arrive with some level of curiosity. Neither wants to feel talked down to.

## Product Purpose

E-commerce + marketing site for a 3D printing startup selling articulated physical toys and custom prints. Made in Indonesia. Direct purchase on-site plus links to Tokopedia/Shopee. Custom order flow for bespoke prints.

Success looks like: first-time visitor understands in 5 seconds what the product is, feels something (delight or confidence), and either adds to cart or submits a custom request.

## Brand Personality

**Serious craft. Playful soul.**

Three words: Bold, Precise, Playful.

Not a toy brand that happens to be serious. Not a professional shop that happens to sell toys. The tension between those two is the identity. The products are technically impressive AND genuinely fun. The site should hold both without resolving the tension.

Voice is direct and confident. No fluff. Copy earns its place. Indonesian-first (the primary audience is local), English feels natural too.

References:

- **Lego.com**: Product-first showcase, playful photography, grid that feels alive not flat
- **Linear.app**: Precision, clean, every pixel intentional, confident without being cold
- **Supreme**: Bold, direct, no explanations, the design IS the brand statement
- **Kith**: Collector culture elevated, product detail that rewards attention

## Anti-references

- Generic Tokopedia/Shopee marketplace clone: no-personality grid, blue/orange noise, badge overload
- SaaS dashboard aesthetic: clean feature-list cards, corporate confidence, zero fun
- Cheap dropship template: cluttered, low trust, "SALE" stickers everywhere
- Forgettable small-business Squarespace: pretty but instant-scrollable, nothing memorable

## Design Principles

1. **Hold the tension.** Serious enough for a collector. Fun enough for a kid's birthday gift. Never resolve to one side. Every page should feel both.
2. **Products are the stars.** The UI frames the product, never competes. Negative space, confident typography, minimal chrome.
3. **Indonesian-made is a feature.** Local identity surfaces naturally: copy, cultural references, the fact that this is made here. Never generic.
4. **Earn surprise.** One moment per page that's unexpected: a tilt, a chunky shadow, a color hit that shouldn't work but does. Not everywhere. Just enough.
5. **Speed over decoration.** Interactions feel fast. Animations ease out hard. Nothing bounces. Load performance is part of the design.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA as baseline
- Color contrast checks required on all text over colored backgrounds (especially yellow accent)
- Reduced motion: all animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Touch targets minimum 44px (primary mobile audience via phone browsing)
